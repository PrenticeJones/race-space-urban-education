The Issue with LASPD

Back to Main Page

The Los Angeles School Police Department is meant to be dedicated to providing security and policing within and around schools in the Los Angeles Unified District. Yet, through the emergence of school violence school administrators seek to filter problematic behaviors like truancy, loitering and disrespect through historical methods of zero tolerance policies which disproportionately affect students of color. I want to investigate the relationship of how the presence of the Los Angeles School Police Department (LASPD) contributes to the pre-established spatially disciplined atmosphere, as seen through in-school suspensions and expulsions, of urban schools. How does it change the way students feel about themselves and their emotional regulation?
